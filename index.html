§01 · Philosophy

Cashflow over speculation.

The focus is on hard assets — energy, shipping, mining and midstream pipelines — sectors that translate real economic activity into reliable dividends. Yields of 6–15 % are not the exception, they are the outcome of concrete cashflow logic.

A tanker earns money when it moves oil. A pipeline gets paid when gas flows through it. A mine generates revenue when copper leaves the ground. 80 % of the portfolio sits in stable, cashflow-rich dividend payers — 20 % stays in cash for counter-cyclical buys during weakness.

§08 · FAQ

Frequently asked.

What are hard assets and why do they pay high dividends?
Hard assets are physical, tangible businesses — ships, pipelines, mines, oil wells, infrastructure. They generate cashflow from real operations: a tanker earns freight rates, a pipeline collects tariffs, a mine produces metals. Because value comes from physical activity (not growth hopes), they distribute more cash — typically 6–15% yields.
What is your investment philosophy?
80/20: 80% in stable high-dividend hard-asset stocks — pipelines, mining royalties, shipping, infrastructure. 20% in cash/ultrashort bonds for tactical deployment. Focus is always on cashflow, never price speculation.
