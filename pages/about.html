My Investor Journey: From Zero to a Hard Asset Strategy

2022: Getting Started — Frustration with Growth Stocks

In 2022, I started investing systematically — like most people at first: ETFs, growth stocks, tech hype. Then interest rates rose sharply. Growth stocks crashed 60–80%. My portfolio along with them. That experience was the turning point: I no longer wanted to depend on price appreciation. I wanted companies that send me money every month and every quarter, regardless of what the market does.

2023: Discovering Hard Assets

The search for real, physically secured cashflow led me to hard assets: shipping stocks (tankers earn money as long as crude oil is transported), midstream pipelines (Enbridge and others collect throughput fees regardless of oil prices), mining royalties (Franco-Nevada receives revenue from mines without operating them). These business models fascinated me: they pay dividends from real economic activity, not from accounting tricks.

2024: Building the Platform & Research Community

I began sharing my research on YouTube — and quickly noticed that many investors ask the same question: "How do I find stocks that pay truly sustainable dividends?" MB Capital Strategies was created as the answer: a platform with real analyses, real numbers, real positions. No affiliate-driven hype, no buy recommendations — just research.

2025–2026: Scaling & Deepening

Today the portfolio spans a five-figure sum, allocated across shipping (TORM, Frontline), midstream (Enbridge, ONEOK), mining (Franco-Nevada, royalty companies) and BDCs (Ares Capital). The platform has grown to 89+ articles, 34 stock analyses and 8 purpose-built financial calculators — all freely accessible. As a contributor on Investing.com, I also reach an international investor audience.

My Approach: Cashflow Over Capital Gains

Most retail investors chase price appreciation. I invest differently: cashflow first. Dividends from hard assets — from companies that move real, physical goods — form the core of my portfolio.

Why Hard Assets?

Energy gets transported, commodities get mined, ships sail — regardless of what Mr. Market thinks on any given day. This economic reality provides inflation protection and delivers predictable distributions. My Hard Assets Guide explains the four core categories with concrete metrics.

The 80/20 Rule

80% of my focus goes to stable, predictable dividend payers — midstream pipelines, infrastructure REITs, mining royalties. 20% goes to more cyclical high-dividend positions like tanker & shipping stocks, which deliver outsized returns during up-cycles.
