On normalised 2026 figures the stock trades at EV/EBITDAX 3.0–3.5x – cheap by any UK peer comparison.

1. What is Serica Energy?

Serica Energy. AIM-listed in London, 228 employees. UK E&P operator focused on the UK Continental Shelf. In 2018 the company bought the Bruce, Keith and Rhum fields (BKR) from BP – gas-heavy, cash-generative. Then in 2023 the Tailwind acquisition: Triton FPSO, Orlando field, Gannet. A small gas producer became a balanced oil-and-gas producer with over 40,000 boepd.

Then 2025: four more acquisitions in one year. The key one – the Greater Laggan Area (40 % operated interest) from TotalEnergies, West of Shetland. The remarkable part: TotalEnergies paid Serica USD 56 million to take the assets on. A negative purchase price. Serica acquires assets and gets paid to do it.

Across all 2025 acquisitions, Serica averaged USD 3.30 per barrel of 2P reserves. Industry average: USD 8 to 12 per barrel. That is consistent contrarian buying – UK North Sea is politically unpopular, which is exactly what keeps asset prices low.

2. The Disaster Year 2025 – and Why It Does Not Change the Thesis

2025 was rough. Revenue fell 17 % to USD 601 million. Production dropped from 34,600 to 27,600 boepd. Net loss: USD 52 million. Sounds like a write-off candidate.

The cause was a single event: the Triton FPSO suffered a major breakdown. Months of zero oil throughput from the Triton fields. Fixed costs kept running while production stopped. Opex per barrel jumped from USD 19 in H1 2024 to USD 37 – nearly doubled.

⚠️ BUT – the USD 52m net loss includes USD 65m of deferred tax charge. Strip that out and Serica was slightly profitable. Pre-tax profit came in at USD 80m (including USD 67m of unrealised hedging gains). This is not a structural problem – it was a one-off operational event.

The dividend was maintained despite the loss year – cut from 23 to 16 GBp, but not scrapped. At USD 210m EBITDAX, a 16 GBp payout represents roughly 30 % of EBITDAX. That is conservative.

4. The EPL – 78 % Tax as Both Problem and Catalyst

Here is the elephant in the room: the Energy Profits Levy. 30 % Corporation Tax + 10 % Supplementary Charge + 38 % EPL = 78 % total tax rate. The OBR expects it to remain until at least March 2030.

That is why UK North Sea stocks are cheap. Not because the assets are bad. Not because the oil is gone. It is British tax policy.

At the same time, the EPL is the single biggest potential catalyst. On 4 March 2026, UK operators met with Chancellor Reeves to discuss reform. OEUK (the UK industry body) is pushing for a price mechanism at 35 % above a price threshold. My estimate: 30 to 40 % probability of reform in 2026, rising to 60 % for 2027.

An EPL reform from 78 % to ~40 % would nearly triple after-tax free cash flow. That is the real upside case.

One more thing: Serica holds tax assets worth over USD 1 billion at nominal value. These shield a meaningful portion of cash taxes – the actual tax bite is less severe than the 78 % headline suggests.

5. Dividend and Yield on Cost

Dividend history: from 3.5 GBp in 2021 to 17 GBp (2022), 23 GBp (2023 and 2024), and 16 GBp for FY2025. The final dividend of 10 GBp is due on 24 July 2026 (subject to AGM approval). Serica also ran a GBP 15m buyback programme in 2024.

At the current price of 277 GBp, the yield is ~5.8 %. Solid, not spectacular.

Marco’s entry: 145 GBp. The calculation is simple:

YOC = 11.0 %
16 GBp dividend ÷ 145 GBp entry price – well above the 8 % quality threshold

If Serica raises the dividend back towards 20 to 23 GBp on normalised 2026 earnings – plausible at EBITDAX of USD 450m+ – the YOC at entry climbs to 14 to 16 %.

My take: The position is 50 shares, roughly GBP 140. A starter position. On any pullback below 240 GBp I add to at least GBP 200. The dividend justifies holding; the EPL catalyst justifies the speculation.

6. Risks – Be Clear-Eyed

Top 5 Risks:
  1. EPL stays at 78 % through 2030 – probability 50 %. Caps upside sharply.
  2. Triton FPSO fails again – probability 25 %. 2025 showed what that costs.
  3. Brent crashes below USD 65 – probability 20 %. Cycles turn.
  4. Rhum field: Iranian JV partner – sanctions risk, 15 %.
  5. Integrating four acquisitions at once – execution risk, 20 %.

Worst case: EPL stays AND Brent falls below USD 65. Target price: 150 to 200 GBp. That is minus 28 to 46 % from current levels. Probability: 10 to 15 %.
